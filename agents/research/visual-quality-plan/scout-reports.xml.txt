# Sky & Atmosphere — Visual Exploration Report

## 1. Current-state assessment

The sky system is technically mature and perf-disciplined: a phase-blended 4-stop gradient with weather-coupled "bruised storm" palettes (`AtmosphereState.js:475`, #29 shipped), real astronomical moon phases (`AtmosphereState.js:655`), 90 deterministic stars + constellations + 12 live twinkling stars, parallax cloud sprites, god-rays, and a composed sky frame cached at 1–5 Hz (`SkyRenderer.js:172`). Weather is a deterministic daily timeline with 3-layer parallax rain, winter snow swap, fog bands, forked midpoint-displacement lightning with a thunder event, and traveling rain curtains; session health already nudges weather via `MoodService`/`VillageDirector` → `applyWeatherEventInfluence` (`AtmosphereState.js:587`, #8 shipped), and day/night reward pairs (aurora/sky-flare, shooting-star/sun-glint) are wired to push/subagent events (#6 shipped). The honest weaknesses: **(a)** at default camera framing the map covers ~80–90% of the viewport, so the sky is a thin sliver — celestial bodies clamp to just above the map edge (`SkyRenderer.js:644`) and read as ground props (in `performance-world-1600x1000.png` the sun looks like a glowing boulder sitting on the stone wall, rays invisible); **(b)** all hero-moment rewards (aurora, shooting stars, sky-flare) render inside the base sky pass *under the world* (`WorldFrameRenderer.js:71`), so they are geometrically occluded exactly where the village sits; **(c)** the night sky is sparse — 90 fixed 1–2px stars regardless of viewport (the 1440×900 night shot shows ~3 faint dots in the visible sliver); **(d)** `SkyRenderer` duplicates `WeatherRenderer` rain/fog with a per-frame background veil that costs ~1k–2.4k individual `stroke()` calls and can slant *against* the foreground rain.

# Terrain & Water — Visual Exploration Report

## 1. Current-state assessment

Terrain is a six-sheet Wang 4-bit tileset system (`claudeville/src/presentation/character-mode/TerrainTileset.js:18`) baked once into a single-surface cache with seasonal ground decals, district washes, forest-floor tints, and atmospheric perspective; water is authored as polylines + basins with kind/region/depth metadata (`claudeville/src/config/scenery.js:8-179`) and carries a deep stack of shipped dynamic passes (flow streaks, glitter, surf bands, ripples, night reflections — v0.23–v0.25). The single dominant flaw is the **water checkerboard**: deep/shallow classification is peppered per tile, so every water body reads as alternating light-teal/navy diamonds — I confirmed it by running `SceneryEngine` headlessly and dumping the class map (e.g. the lagoon at map rows 5–9 is `slDllDDDDDllll…` — deep and shallow interleaved, not a coherent deep center). Root causes: overlapping basins/polylines overwrite each other's depth (last write wins, `SceneryEngine.js:279-286`), deep thresholds are per-shape with per-tile white-noise jitter, and `_drawTerrainTone` alternates two very different fills on `seed > 0.5` (`IsometricRenderer.js:7904,7912`). Secondary weaknesses: shallow tiles treat deep neighbors as *land* in the Wang mask (`IsometricRenderer.js:8930`), drawing sandy shore fringes *inside* water bodies; the noise source `_tileNoise` (`IsometricRenderer.js:1286`) is a per-tile white hash, so every "broad variation" feature (region tint, road materials, vegetation, sea bands) scatters as polka dots instead of clumps; and same-mask interiors repeat one identical 32×32 cell across every tile, so big grass/water expanses go flat. Bridges and the baked landmark span are genuinely good; the shore band, roads, and open sea are serviceable but mechanical.

## 2. Improvement opportunities

Also flip `_sameTerrainClass` for `terrain.shore-shallow` to count deep tiles as same-class (`IsometricRenderer.js:8930` — drop the `!deep`), so mid-water sand fringes vanish and the `shallow-deep` sheet owns the shallow→deep transition. Depth is visual-only (walkability uses `waterTiles`+bridge flags, `SceneryEngine.js:730-742`), so this is safe. Why: fixes the #1 eyesore visible in every world screenshot; the lagoon, river, and sea finally read as bodies of water with depth structure. Effort **M**, risk **med** (threshold tuning per region; `npm run world:validate-terrain` + visual diff). Payoff: **coherence**. No per-frame cost (init-time BFS over ≤1600 tiles).
